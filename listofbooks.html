Favorite Books

I like books, but here are some of my favorites!

  • Harry Potter series
  • Adventure on Trains series
  • Alex Rider series
  • Bumpfizzle The Best On Planet Earth
  • The Danger Gang
  • Curse Of Hallapicktu, A Puzzle Adventure Book
  • Sherlock Holmes And The Disapearing Diamond
  • Flora & Ulysses

Why don't you read these if you haven't? Keep on reading, readers! ⚠️WARNING! CONTAINS SPOILERS!⚠️


- DESCRIPTION FOR HARRY POTTER SERIES -


A very magical plot with fasinating characters. Orphan (and protagonist) Harry learns he is a wizard on his 11th birthday when Hagrid escorts him to magic-teaching Hogwarts School. As a baby, his mother's love protected him and vanquished the villain Voldemort, leaving the child famous as "The Boy who Lived." With his friends Hermione and Ron, Harry has to defeat the returned "He Who Must Not Be Named."


- DESCRIPTION FOR ADVENTURE ON TRAINS SERIES -


A train travelling mystery series. Harrison Beck reluctantly joins his travel-writer Uncle Nat for many train rides. During each train lines, a mystery arises. As suspicions and accusations increase sharply high among the passengers, Harrison investigates, uncovering a few surprises (and friends) along the way. Can he solve every mystery and catch the culprit before they reach every end of the train journey? Breathless train journeys full of deceptions, puzzles and clues to solve.


- DESCRIPTION FOR ALEX RIDER SERIES -


Alex Rider is a series of spy novels by British author Anthony Horowitz. The series revolves around a teenage spy named Alex Rider and is primarily marketed for young adults. The series comprises thirteen novels, as well as six graphic novels, seven short stories, and a supplementary book.


- DESCRIPTION FOR BUMPFIZZLE THE BEST ON PLANET EARTH -


Bumpfizzle is an alien, sent to Earth from Planet Plonk on a research mission. Or is he really just a ten-year-old boy who is feeling a bit disgruntled at all the attention his parents are lavishing on The Baby and makes up his imagination to entertain himself? It is up to readers (well, technically you because you, MAYBE, are reading it) to make up their own minds.


- DESCRIPTION FOR THE DANGER GANG -


From the author of The Christmasaurus, The Danger Gang is a thrilling, funny tale of cursed lightning strikes and transformed children. About an normal ordinary town, with ordinary children and ordinary (protagonist) Franky Brown, whom just moved in. But everything changes after the storm, when strange green lightning and powerful thunder crash down on the town.


- DESCRIPTION FOR THE CURSE OF HALLAPICKTU, A PUZZLE ADVENTURE BOOK -


A brilliant mix of fact, fiction and fun, children can venture through forests, sail the seas and blast into deep space, solving brain-squeezing puzzles along the way. Oscar, Siddiq, Rosalie and Delilah recieve a letter from Aunty Aquilah (and delivered by her cat Humboldt). She's been trapped in her Catacombs of Curiosity by the old spirit of Emperor Hallapicktu, a scholar who loved puzzles as much as Aunty Aquilah! Will the four manage to save Aunty Aquilah and rescue the Catacombs? Or will they fail, only to leave the Catacombs scattered and Aunty Aquilah trapped (And Siddiq free to grab all the snacks in her fridge)? Recommended for children who love brain-twisting puzzles and short, comical reading.


- DESCRIPTION FOR SHERLOCK HOLMES AND THE DISAPPEARING DIAMOND -


When John Watson is accepted at Baker Street Academy, it’s elementary that he’ll become friends with classmates Martha Hudson and Sherlock Holmes. The three have already started palling around when their class witnesses a robbery (“flash rob”) of a valuable diamond while on a field trip. For the remainder of the story, John is trying to figure out what happened; Sherlock, of course, is always several steps ahead of him. But will they be able to catch the thief before home time?


- DESCRIPTION FOR FLORA & ULYSSES -


Flora and Ulysses will defend the defenseless, vanquish villians and protect the weak! Or something. The Illuminated Adventures is a children's novel by American author Kate DiCamillo and illustrated by K.G. Campbell, published in 2013 by Candlewick Press. It tells the story of Flora Belle Buckman and a squirrel named Ulysses (and the book begins with a vaccum cleaner. Yes - a vaccum cleaner.).
